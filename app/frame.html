Grid: 4 / 8 / 12

4 columns mobile, 8 tablet, 12 desktop — the column count itself changes, not just margin and gutter. That means "half" and "quarter" are semantic classes, not fixed span numbers: .col-half is 2 of 4 on mobile, 4 of 8 on tablet, 6 of 12 on desktop — always exactly half. Resize the window to watch the columns, margin, and gutter all shift together at 600px and 1024px. Toggle the grid button (bottom right) to overlay it on this page.

Measure check

Body text at 16px targets a 60–75 character line. On desktop, full-width (12 columns) runs too long to read comfortably — .col-half lands closer to the right measure. Use .prose alongside it for any real paragraph so the cap holds even if the column gets wider.

This paragraph sits in .col-half and is capped with .prose so it stays around a 65-character measure regardless of how wide its column gets. It should read comfortably at every breakpoint without needing a media query to fix it.
